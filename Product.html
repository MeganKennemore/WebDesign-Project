Offered Services

Care options

We have two options that you can choose from when deciding on care for your pets. If you'd like a cheaper option, one of our petsitters can visit daily, for which we charge hourly. If you would like more intense care, our petsitters will move into your residence for the duration of your absence, caring for your pets in their familiar environment. This is a more expensive option, but if your pets have poor health, or need extra attention, we feel it's certainly worth it! In-house petsitters act similarly to housesitters, staying in your home and taking care of your pets as you would. This is a very good option for animals that have trauma and need a stable environment.

Special notes:

For both hourly and in-house sitters, there are a few things we need from you! You need to supply your pets' food, harness and leash, medication, and the info for their vet office. We will also require some info about your pet's normal behavior, routine, and favored toys, in order to give them the best care. You will give some of this info when you book a petsitter, but we also like to have a written copy left for us!

Restrictions on hourly care

We do place some restrictions on hourly care. We will not perform hourly care for infant puppies, kittens, or birds. We will perform hourly care if your pet has minor health requirements, but for major health needs we urge you to book a petsitter for in-house care. For more detailed information on what health issues we are equipped to care for, check our help page.

Don't worry though, your pet will still receive the same amount of care from our hourly petsitters as they would from our in-house petsitters! We will still excercise, feed, cuddle, and play with your pet! Hourly care is an excellent option if your pet doesn't need special medical or social care.
